Product Track

What yalp does from the user's seat. Full detail in product-vision.md and product-spec.md.

You drive yalp with plain language, not a fixed keyword list: you say something, it works out what you mean, and it acts and/or reports back. The honest mental model вҖ” and the scope guardrail вҖ” is "a clever talking pet that sees, chats, and moves toward things, not a butler that fetches." Capabilities sort into three tiers by how they feel: snappy local reflexes (follow, come here, back up, spin, stop, don't-hit-things), cloud-powered talk & visual Q&A ("what do you see?", "read this sign", tell a story), and a clumsy-but-real explore-and-report ("go around the corner and tell me what's there").

v1 is deliberately scoped for the builder's learning, not yet the son's delight: cardboard is fine, and looks, durability, and child-safety are explicit non-goals deferred to a later, sturdier robot-pet build that reuses v1's brain. The headline product calls:

  • DECISION v1 is text-first; voice (STT in, TTS out) is a deferred, separate track so the audio rabbit hole never blocks the core loop. product-vision.md
  • DECISION Reactive movement plus talk & visual Q&A are the heart of v1 вҖ” nail those two and v1 is already a success. product-spec.md
  • DECISION Explore-and-report ships in its clumsy, stop-go form; smooth continuous narration is a named post-v1 stretch goal. product-spec.md
  • DECISION Named-place navigation, manipulation, and stairs/rough-terrain/off-WiFi are cut вҖ” and yalp must decline gracefully instead of flailing. product-vision.md
Tier 1 вҖ” reactive, instant, local Tier 2 вҖ” talk & VQA, a beat Tier 3 вҖ” explore & report, stop-go

Technical Track

How it's built. Full detail in architecture.md, software-spec.md, hardware.md, and roadmap.md.

yalp runs on two control loops with different clocks. A fast reactive layer lives entirely on the Pi (~10вҖ“30 Hz, no network) and owns the motors and safety in real time; a slow deliberative layer in the cloud (~seconds/step) turns your words plus one still photo into a high-level intent. The cloud is an LLM agent handed a bounded menu of robot abilities вҖ” most of which are modes for the reactive layer, not direct motor twitches вҖ” which is what makes "do whatever I tell it" tractable on cheap hardware. The two loops exchange exactly two message shapes (RobotState up, Intent down), and collision-stop sits at the very top of the reactive tick where no command can out-vote it.

The build strategy is brain-on-the-laptop-first behind a hardware-abstraction seam: only motor-control and camera-capture ever cross over to the Pi. The bot is a ~$335 project (the ~$200 brain вҖ” a CanaKit Pi 5 kit + Logitech C270 вҖ” is already ordered), with a cardboard chassis, zero soldering, and two separate power supplies sharing a common ground. The headline technical calls:

  • DECISION Split control into a local fast reactive layer and a cloud slow deliberative layer вҖ” the single most important architectural decision; everything follows from it. architecture.md
  • DECISION Collision-stop is a software priority at the top of the reactive loop (plus a watchdog); a hardware cutoff is deferred to the kid version. architecture.md
  • DECISION Stills, not video, and tiered models (cheap per-step VLM, big model only on hard asks) keep the cloud cheap and fast enough to be fun. software-spec.md
  • DECISION Skip the AI HAT/NPU (CPU + cloud); differential drive with a DRV8833/TB6612, not an L298N. hardware.md
  • DECISION New intents replace, never queue, and SAFE_STOP is sticky вҖ” the body always reflects the freshest human instruction, and never lurches out of a wall on its own. software-spec.md

Top Risks & Open Questions

The highest-severity items from the risk register in roadmap.md В§4вҖ“В§5. These are where to spend worry first.

Risks ranked by what will actually bite

  • RISK рҹ”ҙ Critical вҖ” The loop-to-loop interface is the hard part and the least specified. If motors and the agent loop are built against different mental models of "a mode," step 5 becomes a rewrite. Mitigation: design the contract first, laptop-only, in parallel. architecture.md В· software-spec.md
  • RISK рҹҹ  High вҖ” Person-following fps on a Pi 5 with no NPU. The follow brain is now built and benchmarked on the laptop (track-by-detection); the open question is whether the Pi hits usable fps on CPU. Mitigation: Gate H in hardware-runbook.md is now a fps benchmark-confirmation before follow runs on hardware. software-spec.md
  • RISK рҹҹ  High вҖ” A single front ultrasonic is thin. Narrow cone, side/rear blind spots, silent failure on glass and angled surfaces. Mitigation: define collision-stop priority + post-collision behavior; budget 2вҖ“3 sensors. hardware.md
  • RISK рҹҹ  High вҖ” No encoders = open-loop control. Distance and heading are guesses; the robot drifts and "back up 30 cm" is dead reckoning. Mitigation: accept open-loop for v1; lean on vision feedback in follow mode. hardware.md
  • RISK рҹҹ  High вҖ” Power / brownout. Motor inrush and flyback drag the shared rail down and the Pi reboots вҖ” the #1 cause of mysterious resets. Mitigation: separate supplies + common ground, caps, and a bring-up gate before motors run under the Pi. hardware.md

Open questions on the critical path

  • OPEN Final sensor count вҖ” one front HC-SR04 for v1, or budget 2вҖ“3 (front + corners) to cover the blind spots from the start? hardware.md
  • DECISION Follow-mode tracker вҖ” settled. Track-by-detection (face / hog / person [cv2.dnn] / auto), built and benchmarked on the laptop; remaining question is only the Pi fps at Gate H. software-spec.md
  • OPEN Model / provider choice вҖ” which VLM for the per-step loop vs. the escalation model, and what's the tiering threshold? software-spec.md
  • OPEN Voice timing & far-field STT вҖ” when does the voice track start, and is noisy far-field speech in scope for v1 or punted to the kid version? product-spec.md
  • OPEN How much should v1 anticipate the kid version? Carry the software/brain forward, treat the body as throwaway вҖ” but where exactly is the line? product-vision.md
  • OPEN Post-collision behavior вҖ” after a collision-stop fires, back off, rotate to find clear space, or halt and ask the brain? architecture.md
