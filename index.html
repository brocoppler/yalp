Build-and-learn robot · v1

yalp — a clever talking pet that sees, chats & moves

A from-scratch hobby robot — a Raspberry Pi 5 brain, a USB camera, and two differential-drive wheels — that you talk to in plain language. It interprets your intent, moves and looks around under its own power, and tells you what it sees.

This page is the single, self-contained home for the whole spec. The depth lives in seven embedded documents — click any sidebar link or any backticked-filename.md in the text to read it inline, no page ever leaves this file. Below is a skimmable consolidation: what yalp is, the headline decisions on each track, and the risks most likely to bite.

Product Track

What yalp does from the user's seat. Full detail in product-vision.md and product-spec.md.

You drive yalp with plain language, not a fixed keyword list: you say something, it works out what you mean, and it acts and/or reports back. The honest mental model — and the scope guardrail — is "a clever talking pet that sees, chats, and moves toward things, not a butler that fetches." Capabilities sort into three tiers by how they feel: snappy local reflexes (follow, come here, back up, spin, stop, don't-hit-things), cloud-powered talk & visual Q&A ("what do you see?", "read this sign", tell a story), and a clumsy-but-real explore-and-report ("go around the corner and tell me what's there").

v1 is deliberately scoped for the builder's learning, not yet the son's delight: cardboard is fine, and looks, durability, and child-safety are explicit non-goals deferred to a later, sturdier robot-pet build that reuses v1's brain. The headline product calls:

  • DECISION v1 is text-first; voice (STT in, TTS out) is a deferred, separate track so the audio rabbit hole never blocks the core loop. product-vision.md
  • DECISION Reactive movement plus talk & visual Q&A are the heart of v1 — nail those two and v1 is already a success. product-spec.md
  • DECISION Explore-and-report ships in its clumsy, stop-go form; smooth continuous narration is a named post-v1 stretch goal. product-spec.md
  • DECISION Named-place navigation, manipulation, and stairs/rough-terrain/off-WiFi are cut — and yalp must decline gracefully instead of flailing. product-vision.md
Tier 1 — reactive, instant, local Tier 2 — talk & VQA, a beat Tier 3 — explore & report, stop-go

Technical Track

How it's built. Full detail in architecture.md, software-spec.md, hardware.md, and roadmap.md.

yalp runs on two control loops with different clocks. A fast reactive layer lives entirely on the Pi (~10–30 Hz, no network) and owns the motors and safety in real time; a slow deliberative layer in the cloud (~seconds/step) turns your words plus one still photo into a high-level intent. The cloud is an LLM agent handed a bounded menu of robot abilities — most of which are modes for the reactive layer, not direct motor twitches — which is what makes "do whatever I tell it" tractable on cheap hardware. The two loops exchange exactly two message shapes (RobotState up, Intent down), and collision-stop sits at the very top of the reactive tick where no command can out-vote it.

The build strategy is brain-on-the-laptop-first behind a hardware-abstraction seam: only motor-control and camera-capture ever cross over to the Pi. The bot is a ~$335 project (the ~$200 brain — a CanaKit Pi 5 kit + Logitech C270 — is already ordered), with a cardboard chassis, zero soldering, and two separate power supplies sharing a common ground. The headline technical calls:

  • DECISION Split control into a local fast reactive layer and a cloud slow deliberative layer — the single most important architectural decision; everything follows from it. architecture.md
  • DECISION Collision-stop is a software priority at the top of the reactive loop (plus a watchdog); a hardware cutoff is deferred to the kid version. architecture.md
  • DECISION Stills, not video, and tiered models (cheap per-step VLM, big model only on hard asks) keep the cloud cheap and fast enough to be fun. software-spec.md
  • DECISION Skip the AI HAT/NPU (CPU + cloud); differential drive with a DRV8833/TB6612, not an L298N. hardware.md
  • DECISION New intents replace, never queue, and SAFE_STOP is sticky — the body always reflects the freshest human instruction, and never lurches out of a wall on its own. software-spec.md

Top Risks & Open Questions

The highest-severity items from the risk register in roadmap.md §4–§5. These are where to spend worry first.

Risks ranked by what will actually bite

  • RISK 🔴 Critical — The loop-to-loop interface is the hard part and the least specified. If motors and the agent loop are built against different mental models of "a mode," step 5 becomes a rewrite. Mitigation: design the contract first, laptop-only, in parallel. architecture.md · software-spec.md
  • RISK 🔴 Critical — Person-following on a Pi 5 with no NPU. A real person detector may not hit usable fps on CPU, so follow-mode could stutter and lose a moving target. Mitigation: an early fps spike as a go/no-go gate before any follow code. software-spec.md
  • RISK 🟠 High — A single front ultrasonic is thin. Narrow cone, side/rear blind spots, silent failure on glass and angled surfaces. Mitigation: define collision-stop priority + post-collision behavior; budget 2–3 sensors. hardware.md
  • RISK 🟠 High — No encoders = open-loop control. Distance and heading are guesses; the robot drifts and "back up 30 cm" is dead reckoning. Mitigation: accept open-loop for v1; lean on vision feedback in follow mode. hardware.md
  • RISK 🟠 High — Power / brownout. Motor inrush and flyback drag the shared rail down and the Pi reboots — the #1 cause of mysterious resets. Mitigation: separate supplies + common ground, caps, and a bring-up gate before motors run under the Pi. hardware.md

Open questions on the critical path

  • OPEN Final sensor count — one front HC-SR04 for v1, or budget 2–3 (front + corners) to cover the blind spots from the start? hardware.md
  • OPEN Follow-mode tracker choice — full person detector, lightweight blob/color tracker, or track-by-detection hybrid? Pending the fps spike. software-spec.md
  • OPEN Model / provider choice — which VLM for the per-step loop vs. the escalation model, and what's the tiering threshold? software-spec.md
  • OPEN Voice timing & far-field STT — when does the voice track start, and is noisy far-field speech in scope for v1 or punted to the kid version? product-spec.md
  • OPEN How much should v1 anticipate the kid version? Carry the software/brain forward, treat the body as throwaway — but where exactly is the line? product-vision.md
  • OPEN Post-collision behavior — after a collision-stop fires, back off, rotate to find clear space, or halt and ask the brain? architecture.md
